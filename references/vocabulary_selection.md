# Vocabulary Selection Guide

Select the vocabulary and phrases the creator is trying to teach, not every unfamiliar word.

## Default Quantity

- Choose 15-25 items for a normal 8-25 minute lesson.
- Choose 8-12 items for a short video.
- Choose up to 30 items only when the video is explicitly a long vocabulary lesson.

## Prioritize Items With Teaching Intent

Choose an item when the creator:

- Defines it, translates it, or explains nuance.
- Repeats it several times.
- Gives examples, corrections, or pronunciation guidance.
- Contrasts it with another expression.
- Uses it in the title, chapter headings, screen text, or lesson framing.
- Shows it as a phrase, collocation, pattern, or sentence frame learners can reuse.

## Prefer High-Value Language Chunks

Prioritize:

- Phrasal verbs and idioms.
- Collocations, such as `make a decision`, `highly recommend`, or `sound natural`.
- Sentence frames, such as `I was wondering if...` or `What I mean is...`.
- Discourse markers, such as `actually`, `basically`, `to be honest`, or `here's the thing`.
- Pronunciation targets, such as linking, stress, reductions, or commonly confused sounds.

## Avoid Low-Value Extraction

Do not select an item just because it is:

- Rare but irrelevant to the lesson.
- A proper noun, brand name, or video-specific detail.
- A basic function word with no teaching focus.
- Repeated only because it is part of unrelated examples.
- Too broad to explain usefully, such as `get` without a phrase or pattern.

## Ranking Heuristic

When too many candidates appear, rank them by:

1. Explicit teaching emphasis.
2. Reusability for learners.
3. Frequency in the transcript.
4. Nuance or mistake-prevention value.
5. Connection to the video's title or stated goal.

## Output Expectations

For each selected item, include:

- The English item exactly as learners should remember it.
- IPA for the headword or main phrase where practical.
- Chinese meaning in this video's context.
- A short explanation of usage or nuance.
- One original sentence from the transcript or a faithful short quote.
- One new learner-friendly example sentence.
- Common collocations, grammar pattern, or a note about register.
